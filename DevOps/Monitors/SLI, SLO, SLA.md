

Here’s a **crisp, no-fluff explanation**:

---

### 🔹 **SLI (Service Level Indicator)**

> **What you measure**

- Actual metric from system
    
- Example:
    
    - Response time = 200ms
        
    - Uptime = 99.5%
        

---

### 🔹 **SLO (Service Level Objective)**

> **What you aim for**

- Target/goal for the metric
    
- Example:
    
    - 99.9% uptime
        
    - 95% requests < 300ms
        

---

### 🔹 **SLA (Service Level Agreement)**

> **What you promise (with consequences)**

- Contract with users/customers
    
- If not met → penalties / credits
    
- Example:
    
    - “99.9% uptime guaranteed or refund”
        

---

## 🧠 One-line mapping

- **SLI** → measurement
    
- **SLO** → target
    
- **SLA** → commitment
    

---

## ⚡ Quick analogy

- SLI → “Current speed = 60 km/h”
    
- SLO → “Try to stay above 50 km/h”
    
- SLA → “If speed drops below 50 → pay fine”
