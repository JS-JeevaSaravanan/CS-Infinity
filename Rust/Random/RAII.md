

RAII stands for Resource Acquisition Is Initialization.

It is a programming idiom, primarily associated with C++, where the acquisition of a resource (like memory, file handles, mutexes, etc.) is tied to the initialization of an object, and the release of that resource is tied to the object's destruction. This ensures that resources are automatically managed, even in the presence of exceptions or multiple exit points in a function. The resource is acquired in the constructor of a class and released in its destructor.
